Section 01

Where You Are Today

Your business has a strong foundation. You have an established client base, a solid network of referring agents, and a team that knows the work inside out. The processes you have in place got you to where you are today.

Currently, the operation runs across a combination of physical books, spreadsheets, a whiteboard, OneDrive, and Google Calendar. Quotes are written up on-site and processed manually. Calendar entries are created by hand. Agent enquiries come through as phone calls and texts. Photos taken at properties are transferred to OneDrive between appointments. Listing statuses on realestate.com are checked manually to track what has sold or gone under offer.

The team manages a high volume of inbound communication from agents. The office handles scheduling, but agents often reach out directly as well, which adds to the daily workload. Follow-ups on open quotes can be difficult to stay on top of during busy periods with back-to-back appointments across the city.

There is limited visibility at the moment on metrics like quote conversion rates, which agents are generating the most work, or how many leads come in that never get quoted. These are insights that become more valuable as the business continues to grow.

The opportunity is not about fixing what is broken. It's about giving the business the infrastructure to handle the volume it's already doing more efficiently, and to scale from here without adding more manual work to the plate.

Section 04

Is It Worth It?

Let us put some numbers around it. We will use a conservative baseline of $1,000,000 in annual revenue. If your business does more than that, the maths only gets better.

01
Lost Jobs
How many leads or jobs do you think slip through the cracks each month? Not because anyone is doing anything wrong - just because it's manual and things get busy. An agent texts during a back-to-back quoting day and it gets buried. A follow-up does not happen because the next install took priority. Even if it's one or two a month, multiply that by your average job value, then multiply by twelve. That is your first number.
The reality is, right now there is no way to even know the answer to that question. Without a system tracking what comes in versus what gets quoted, the leakage is invisible. You cannot fix what you cannot see.
On a $1M business, even a conservative 5% leakage rate is $50,000 a year in revenue you probably never knew you lost.
02
Admin Hours
How many hours a week does your team spend on work this system would do automatically? Building quotes, copying details into Xero, updating spreadsheets, sending follow-up emails, chasing access info, updating calendars, printing packing lists, manually checking listing statuses online. Whatever that number is, multiply it by what that time costs you. That is your second number.
At $40/hour loaded cost, 15-20 hours a week is $30,000 - $40,000 a year on tasks this system does automatically.
